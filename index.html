ABOUT THE CHALLENGE

Every October, the community of Abbeyfeale comes together for one of Limerick's most inspiring charity events.

The Dromtrasna Challenge is more than just a run - it's a celebration of community spirit, determination, and giving back. Since 2014, we've welcomed thousands of participants from across Ireland to our scenic routes through the beautiful West Limerick countryside.

Starting and finishing at Dromtrasna National School, participants of all ages and abilities can take part in our chip-timed competitive runs or enjoy a leisurely walk with family and friends.

Supporting Our Community

All proceeds go to two vital local healthcare services:

Milford Care Centre

Providing specialist palliative care and support to patients and families across the Mid-West region.

St. Ita's Community Hospital

Delivering essential healthcare services to the Newcastle West community and surrounding areas.

FAQ'S

On-the-day registration opens at 9:00 AM at Dromtrasna National School. We recommend arriving early to allow time for parking and warm-up. Pre-registration online is strongly encouraged as it's faster and helps us plan better.

Free parking is available at Dromtrasna National School and in the adjacent field. Stewards will direct you on arrival. Please follow their instructions and car pool where possible.

Yes, on-the-day registration is available for all events. However, pre-registration is required to receive a chip for the timed runs. We strongly recommend registering online in advance to guarantee your spot and avoid queues on the morning.

Yes! The 4K Walk is family-friendly and welcomes dogs (on leads) and buggies. Please be mindful of other participants and clean up after your pets.

Yes, water stations are positioned along the 10K and 5K routes. There is also water and refreshments available at the finish line for all participants.

The event goes ahead rain or shine - this is Ireland after all! Please dress appropriately for the weather conditions. In the unlikely event of extreme weather making conditions unsafe, we will communicate via our social media channels.
